Support for creators

Role

While I was a designer at Thinkmill, my colleagues and I formed a cross-functional team to help Vocal scale their business in 2022.

Problem

With the ability to earn money by reads and partake in writing challenges, Vocal attracted many avid writers upon launch. But since writers were the primary users of the platform, growing a readership among other writers was a challenge for many Vocal users.

Method

We published a survey to collect attitudes towards creator support features on Vocal. Once surveys were analysed, 12 focus group sessions were conducted where the Design Lead tested several ideas to see what kinds of creator experiences users were gravitating. I helped with forming the discussion guide, note-taking the sessions and synthesing our findings in a visual chart.

Most of the ideas we tested centred around increasing engagement between writers and readers, boosting content and offering exclusive perks to VIP readers. I sourced the ideas we tested from other apps, user submitted feedback on Vocal and tossed in a few of our team's ideas too.

Reader representation

Since it was a challenge to find readers of the app from the survey, writers who posted 2 or less stories were our closest representatives for that target group. We found that most creators on Vocal were keen to progress their writing skills and publish more stories so understanding readers on Vocal is certainly an area left unexplored.

Pledge to Support

The ability to receive tips on an ongoing basis from supporters tested really well among participants. With per read, one of the key draws of Vocal is the ability for writer's to focus more on writing and less on sharing their writing. Thispay feature fit in naturally with Vocal's offerings to help creators focus on their craft. I helped design the new creator profile pages using the Vocal Design System.

Outcome

I would have measured usage of the pledge support feature.
